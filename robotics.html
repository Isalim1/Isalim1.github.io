Vice-President and Co-Founder of robotics program at Excelsior academy

Starting off as a rookie team and starting late, preparing for the annual robotics competition was difficult. As a team we rigorously worked to make sure we had everything appropriately in place to compete in the competition. As a leader, I was presented with setting a clear vision for the members of the club, motivating and guiding members to a common goal. I led our team to reach semi-finals our first year, proving our dedication against teams that have several years under their belts. My second and last year leading robotics, we placed into the final rounds, once again showing what cooperation between team members and motivation can do against teams with years of experience against us.  Fall of 2014- Fall of 2016



First set of Instructors At Newburgh Unity Center

Opening a new program at the community center teaching students grades 2-8  about computer science drew attention to fellow colleagues interested in teaching at the program. I competed for 1 of 8 openings against 20 fellow peers and was awarded one of the jobs at the center. There, we teach different computer science platforms including Scratch, Dash and Dots, and Lego Mindstorms. Going through 12 week cycles, our current students advance to higher level learning platforms, while 30 more students are introduced to the program. Teaching students basic algorithmic functions of block based programing, I believe we can set the roots for further exploration in the Programming and Robotic engineering field.  September of 2016- Present
