Vice-President and Co-Founder of robotics program at Excelsior academy

During my first year as one of the founding members of the robotics program, we faced many challenges while preparing for the annual regional robotics competition. As a team, we worked rigorously to make sure we had everything in place to compete in the competition. As a leader, it was essential that I set a clear vision for the members of the club, while motivating and guiding them to a common goal. I co-led our team to the semifinals that year, proving our dedication against teams that had several years under their belts. My second and final year leading robotics, we placed into one of the final rounds, once again showing what cooperation between team members and motivation can do. Fall of 2014- Fall of 2016



First set of Instructors At Newburgh Unity Center

When we heard about the new program teaching students about computer science at the community center, many of my colleagues were interested in the program. I competed for one of eight openings against twenty of my peers and was awarded one of the jobs at the center. There, we teach structural programming and coding through different computer science platforms including Scratch, Dash and Dots, and Lego Mindstorms. Going through twelve week cycles, our current students advance to higher-level learning platforms, while thirty more students are introduced to the program. By teaching students basic algorithmic functions of block based programing, I believe we can plant the roots for further exploration in the Programming and Robotic engineering field. September of 2016- Present
