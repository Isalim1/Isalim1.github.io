Vice-President and Co-Founder of robotics program at Excelsior academy

As a founding member of the robotics program, we got off to a late start our first year, adding to the challenges we faced when preparing for the annual robotics competition during our inaugural year. As a team, we rigorously worked to make sure we had everything appropriately in place to compete in the competition. As a leader, I was presented with setting a clear vision for the members of the club, while motivating and guiding them to a common goal. I co-led our team to reach the semi-finals our first year, proving our dedication against teams that had several years under their belts. My second and last year leading robotics, we placed into one of the final rounds, once again showing what cooperation between team members and motivation can do against teams with years of experience. Fall of 2014- Fall of 2016



First set of Instructors At Newburgh Unity Center

Opening a new program at the community center teaching students grades 2-8 about computer science drew attention to fellow colleagues interested in teaching at the program. I competed for 1 of 8 openings against 20 fellow peers and was awarded one of the jobs at the center. There, we teach different computer science platforms including Scratch, Dash and Dots, and Lego Mindstorms. Going through 12 week cycles, our current students advance to higher level learning platforms, while 30 more students are introduced to the program. Teaching students basic algorithmic functions of block based programing, I believe we can set the roots for further exploration in the Programming and Robotic engineering field.
