Project manager of Slavery Awareness Project

In my sophomore year at Excelsior Academy, our 10th grade cohort was split into several groups, each tasked with different activities to inform the public about modern-day slavery, a student-selected topic we had been studying for months. Choosing to make a website using HTML/CSS was a difficult task, but one we were excited to take on. As one of the leaders, I realized the importance of effective communication. While we were working on this project in between classes, it was difficult to manage the workload, while staying motivated to complete the website on time; rushing to tie everything in place towards the end made the project ever-more challenging. As I reflected on this experience afterwards, I recognized the importance of effective communication. September of 2015- February of 2016
Website can be found at Quit Slavery












Part of student advisory board for Global To Local

As a club we are dedicated in serving the community of Newburgh to better help it thrive as a whole. We plan to go to Quito Ecuador in the summer of 2017 to volunteer at an afterschool program assisting children. Volunteering at a grassroots program for the community, we hope to better understand how we can almost replicate the actions that greatly supported their community and apply it back to the Hudson-Valley. Upon our return we will be participating in student-led projects addressing issues around the community. We are currently involved in fundraising for the trip and strengthening our voice to the public. September of 2016- Present
